E-commerce Sales Insights: Enhancing Strategies with Data-Driven Analysis

Abstract

This study examines the relationship between data analytics and e-commerce, emphasizing the significance of procurement trends, dynamic pricing tactics, and geographic demand patterns. Using geographic data and a Flipkart sales dataset, actionable insights and strategic recommendations are provided to optimize pricing, inventory, and marketing decisions. Key findings include significant sales contributions from Delhi and HR-NCR, driven by discounts on affordable products and regional purchasing habits.

Introduction

Problem Statement

Optimizing advertising and promotional strategies is becoming increasingly challenging for e-commerce platforms. This study addresses key concerns about price strategies, inventory planning, and regional demand trends to improve customer satisfaction and revenue.

Impact

  • Marketing Teams: Focus advertising on high-performing regions.
  • Inventory Managers: Align inventory efficiently with demand.
  • Pricing Strategists: Enhance profits with dynamic pricing.

Research Questions

  • How do sales patterns vary across regions?
  • What factors contribute to certain areas driving overall revenue?
  • How can discount strategies be optimized to maximize revenue and retention?
  • What trends in procurement quantities improve inventory planning?
  • How can pricing strategies be optimized to enhance profitability?

Methodology

Data Collection

Data was sourced from the Flipkart sales dataset (Kaggle) and a geospatial dataset (Simple Maps). Key dimensions included city names, product IDs, and dates, while metrics included procured quantity, discount amounts, and total weighted landing prices. Missing values were handled through imputation and validation.

Data Analysis

Tools used:

  • Python: For data preprocessing.
  • Tableau: For visualization.

Techniques included bar charts and scatter plots for trend identification, treemaps for geographical contributions, and time-series analysis for procurement trends.

Key Findings

Sales by Region

68% of sales revenue comes from Delhi and HR-NCR. Mumbai and Bengaluru present significant expansion opportunities.

Pricing and Discounts

Mid-range goods saw significant sales growth due to discounts, with most products priced between 100 Indian rupees.

Procurement Trends

Procurement volumes dropped in April 2022, indicating changing demand. Seasonal peaks align with major sales events, requiring dynamic inventory adjustments.

Implications and Recommendations

  • Marketing: Focus on Delhi and HR-NCR while expanding campaigns in Mumbai and Bengaluru.
  • Pricing: Leverage dynamic pricing strategies to capitalize on demand fluctuations.
  • Inventory Management: Align procurement with region-specific sales trends.

Challenges

Challenges included resolving dataset discrepancies, managing large volumes of data, and integrating various aspects to generate actionable insights.

Conclusion

This study provides a comprehensive analysis of e-commerce sales patterns, offering practical methods for improving pricing, inventory, and promotional strategies. Future research should explore competitor benchmarking and customer demographics to gain a broader perspective.
