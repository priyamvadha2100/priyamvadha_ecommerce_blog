E-commerce Sales Insights: Enhancing Strategies with Data-Driven Analysis

Abstract

This study analyzes how data analytics and e-commerce are related, focusing its significance of regional demand patterns, changing price tactics, and supply trends. The use of geography data and a Flipkart sales dataset produces beneficial findings and suggested strategies that improve pricing, inventory, and promotional actions.

Introduction

Problem Statement

Due to increased competition and changing customer expectations, online retail sites are finding it becoming increasingly hard to reach more buyers and establish a solid brand representation.

Impact

  • Marketing Teams: Prioritize targeting Delhi and HR-NCR, while also increasing efforts to grow campaigns in Mumbai and Bengaluru.
  • Inventory Managers:Utilize flexible pricing strategies to take advantage of changing demand patterns.
  • Pricing Strategists: Enhance profits with dynamic pricing.

Research Questions

  • How do sales patterns vary across regions?
  • What factors contribute to certain areas driving overall revenue?
  • How can discount strategies be optimized to maximize revenue and retention?
  • What trends in procurement quantities improve inventory planning?
  • How can pricing strategies be optimized to enhance profitability?

Methodology

Data Collection

Data was sourced from the Flipkart sales dataset (Kaggle) and a geospatial dataset (Simple Maps). Key dimensions included city names, product IDs, and dates, while metrics included procured quantity, discount amounts, and total weighted landing prices. Missing values were handled through imputation and validation.

Data Analysis

Tools used:

  • Python: For data preprocessing.
  • Tableau: For visualization.

The approaches used were bar charts, histogram, dual axis line graph to identify developments, treemaps for regional contributions, and time-series analysis for procurement trends.

Key Findings

Sales by Region

68% of sales revenue comes from Delhi and HR-NCR. Mumbai and Bengaluru present significant expansion opportunities.

Pricing and Discounts

Due to decreases mid-range products displayed a significant rise in sales; the majority of all these items were priced between 100 Indian rupees.

Procurement Trends

Procurement volumes dropped in April 2022, indicating changing demand. Seasonal peaks align with major sales events, requiring dynamic inventory adjustments.

Implications and Recommendations

  • Marketing:Should focus on expanding campaigns I Mumbai and bengaluru because it has low products being sold.
  • Pricing:Use dynamic pricing methods to profit from changes in demand.
  • Inventory Management: Adapt procurement to sales trends specific to a given location.

Challenges

Managing huge quantities of data, reconciling dataset inequality, and integrating various components to generate useful insights were amongst the difficulties.

Conclusion

This study offers an in-depth investigation of e-commerce sales trends along with useful suggestions for enhancing products, pricing, and marketing tactics. To obtain an expanded perspective, future studies must involve customer demographics and competition comparisons.
